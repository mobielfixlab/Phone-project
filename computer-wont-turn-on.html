Computer won't
turn on?

Before you panic (or buy a new one), run this checklist — it's the same order we check things in at the bench, from most likely to least.

By the Mobile Fix Lab technicians Updated July 2026 5 min read

First, figure out which "won't turn on" you have

  • Totally dead — no lights, no fans, no sounds. → Power problem. Start at step 1.
  • Turns on but no picture — fans spin, lights on, screen black. → Display or boot problem. Skip to step 4.
  • Starts then dies — powers up for seconds, shuts off. → Often thermal or power delivery; see our overheating guide, then step 6.

1. Prove the outlet and cable (everyone skips this)

Plug a lamp into the same outlet. Bypass the power strip and go straight to the wall. On desktops, reseat the power cable at both ends and check the power supply's rocker switch on the back — the number of "dead" desktops fixed by that switch is humbling.

2. Laptops: charger, then a hard reset

Try a known-good charger if you can (the right wattage). Leave it charging for 30 minutes — a deeply drained battery can show nothing at first. Then hold the power button for 15 seconds and try again. No charging light at all with a good charger points to a broken charge port — one of the most common laptop repairs we do.

3. Desktops: listen and look

Press power and pay attention: any click, beep, or fan twitch is information. A pattern of beeps is the motherboard telling you what's wrong (usually RAM or graphics). A faint click-click-click often means the power supply is failing — a very repairable, moderately priced fix.

4. "It's on but the screen is black"

  • Check the monitor: is it on, on the right input, cable snug at both ends? Swap the video cable if you have a spare.
  • Laptop: shine a flashlight at the screen at an angle — if you can faintly see the desktop, the backlight or panel has failed (screen replacement from $99).
  • Plug an external monitor into a laptop; if that works, it's the built-in display, not the computer.

5. Reseat the RAM

If you're comfortable opening the case: power off, unplug, press the clips, pull the RAM sticks and firmly click them back. A drifted RAM stick is a classic black-screen cause, especially after moving the computer.

6. Know when to stop

Stop and bring it in when: you smell burning, you've had liquid anywhere near it, it shuts off instantly every time, or you've reached this line without success. Continuing to poke a power-delivery fault can take out parts that were healthy.
