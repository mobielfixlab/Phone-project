Gaming PC running
way too hot?

Loud fans, FPS drops twenty minutes in, shutdowns mid-match — heat is the culprit, and it's fixable. Here's where it comes from and what actually works.

By the Mobile Fix Lab technicians Updated July 2026 5 min read

How heat steals your frames

When a CPU or GPU gets too hot, it doesn't just suffer quietly — it throttles, deliberately slowing down to survive. That's why a game runs perfectly for twenty minutes and then turns into a slideshow. Push further and the PC shuts off entirely to protect the silicon. Both are symptoms of the same disease.

The usual suspects, in order

1. Dust — the silent killer

A year of gaming pulls a shocking amount of dust through a case. It mats over heatsink fins and fan blades like a wool sweater on your radiator. If you can see gray fuzz through the vents, this is your problem.

2. Dried thermal paste

The paste between chip and cooler dries out in 3–5 years. Once it does, temps climb 10–20°C for no visible reason. If your PC ran cool when new and creeps hotter every season, suspect the paste.

3. Airflow, not fan count

More fans isn't more cooling if the air has nowhere to go: cases shoved against walls, intake vents on carpet, cable spaghetti blocking the path, or all fans blowing the same direction. Air needs a clear front-to-back (or bottom-to-top) route.

4. A failing fan or pump

Bearings wear out. A fan that rattles, clicks, or stops spinning under load — or an AIO water cooler with a dying pump — turns a good cooling setup into a space heater.

Fix it: the order we'd do it in

  1. Check your temps with a free tool (HWMonitor, or the overlay in GeForce/Adrenalin software) so you have a before/after number.
  2. Clean the machine. Power off, take the side panel off, and clear dust from heatsinks and fans — hold fan blades still while cleaning so they don't over-spin.
  3. Fix the airflow. Case off the carpet, a hand's width from the wall, intakes unblocked.
  4. Repaste if it's 3+ years old. This is the step most people skip and the one that brings the biggest drop — but it means removing the cooler (and on GPUs, a full teardown), so know your comfort level.
  5. Replace worn fans — cheap, quick, and quieter afterward.

Keep it cool for good

  • Dust the intakes monthly; deep-clean the inside twice a year.
  • Repaste every 3–5 years (sooner for daily heavy gaming).
  • Leave breathing room around the case — cabinets are for dishes, not PCs.
  • Watch your temps once in a while; a slow 10°C creep is your early warning.
