Is your iPhone battery
quietly dying?

Batteries don't fail overnight — they fade, and iOS hides it well. Here are the five signs yours is done, and the 30-second check that settles it.

By the Mobile Fix Lab technicians Updated July 2026 4 min read

The 30-second check first

Open Settings → Battery → Battery Health (and Charging) and look at Maximum Capacity. That's how much charge your battery holds compared to when it was new. Above 85%: you're fine. Below 80%: the battery is officially degraded and every sign below starts showing up. If you also see "your battery's health is significantly degraded," the phone has already told you.

Sign 1: It dies at 20–30%

A worn battery can't report its charge accurately. The phone thinks it has a quarter tank left, the voltage sags, and it shuts off mid-photo. If your iPhone routinely dies before the percentage hits zero, the battery is lying to you.

Sign 2: Random shutdowns — especially in the cold

Cold weather and heavy load (camera, maps, games) demand quick bursts of power that an old battery can't deliver. The phone protects itself by shutting down. A phone that dies in a winter parking lot but "recovers" indoors is a textbook battery case.

Sign 3: The phone got slower

This is the sneaky one. When a battery can't deliver stable power, iOS deliberately throttles the processor to prevent shutdowns. Apps open slower, animations stutter, and everyone blames the phone's age — when it's the battery. A new cell removes the throttling, which is why a $59 swap often feels like a new phone.

Sign 4: It's hot, or the charge jumps around

Warm during ordinary use (not just charging or gaming), or the percentage leaps from 60% to 45% in minutes — both are signs of an unstable, worn cell.

Sign 5: The screen or back is lifting

⚠️

This one is urgent. A bulging screen or back panel means the battery is swelling with gas. Stop using it, stop charging it, keep it cool, and bring it in — swollen lithium cells are a fire risk and need safe professional removal. We handle them every week.
