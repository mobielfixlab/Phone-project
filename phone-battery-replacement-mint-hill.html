Signs your battery is done

  • Doesn't last through the day like it used to
  • Dies suddenly at 20–30% remaining
  • Shuts off in the cold or under load (camera, games, maps)
  • Phone feels slower — old batteries make phones throttle performance
  • iPhone battery health reads under 80% (Settings → Battery → Battery Health)
  • The phone runs hot while charging, or charge percentage jumps around

Two or more of those and a battery swap will genuinely feel like getting a new phone — for a fraction of the price. Not sure? Read our guide on the 5 signs your iPhone battery needs replacing.

⚠️

Screen lifting or back bulging? That's a swollen battery. Stop using and charging the phone, keep it cool, and bring it in as soon as you can — swollen cells are a fire risk and need safe removal, which we handle every week.

Battery replacement starts at $59 and takes about 30 minutes for most phones. FAQ

Battery replacement questions

How much does a battery replacement cost?

From $59 for iPhone, Samsung and Pixel phones, including the part, fitting and a 90-day warranty. Your exact quote depends on the model — always confirmed in writing before we start.

Will I lose my data?

No. A battery swap doesn't touch your storage — apps, photos and settings stay exactly as they are, and we don't need your passcode for the swap itself.

At what battery health should I replace it?

Below about 80% maximum capacity most phones start throttling and dying early. Under 80% on an iPhone's Battery Health screen is the point where a replacement clearly pays off.

How long does it take?

About 30 minutes for most phones — easy to do while you wait in the shop.
