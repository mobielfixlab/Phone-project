Phone not charging?
Start with the lint.

Most "dead" charging ports aren't dead at all. Here are the seven causes we see, in order of likelihood — from free fixes to the ones worth a trip to the lab.

By the Mobile Fix Lab technicians Updated July 2026 4 min read

1. Lint in the port (the #1 cause, by far)

Every time the phone goes in a pocket, a little lint goes in the port. Over months it compacts into a dense plug at the back, and the cable stops seating fully — it may click in, wiggle, or only charge at certain angles. If your cable doesn't slide in as deep as it used to, this is almost certainly it.

⚠️

Never dig it out with anything metal. Pins, needles and paper clips bend and short the port's tiny contacts — we regularly see free lint problems turned into $69 port replacements. A wooden toothpick, gently, with the phone off, is the only safe DIY tool. Or bring it in: a proper cleaning is quick and often free with our diagnosis.

2. The cable or adapter

Cables fray inside the insulation where you can't see it. Try a different cable and wall adapter you know work — borrow one if you have to. If a different set charges fine, you just saved yourself a repair.

3. A software glitch

Sometimes the phone stops recognizing the charger after an update or a crash. Restart the phone with the charger connected. On iPhone, also check for a "liquid detected" warning — the phone deliberately blocks charging when the port reads wet.

4. Wireless works, cable doesn't

This one's diagnostic gold: if the phone charges on a wireless pad but ignores every cable, the port itself is at fault and the rest of the phone is fine. Cleaning or replacement sorts it.

5. The port is physically damaged

Bent pins, a loose port that wiggles with the cable, or damage from a yank or a fall. The port is soldered to the board, so this is a workshop job — charging port repair starts at $69 at our lab and takes about 40 minutes to an hour, tested at full current before pickup.

6. The battery itself

A battery at the end of its life can refuse to take charge, show wild percentage jumps, or die instantly off the charger. If charging works but the battery misbehaves, it's a battery replacement — from $59, about 30 minutes.

7. Board-level charging faults

Rarest, but real: the charging circuit on the motherboard fails, often after liquid exposure or a knockoff charger. Signs include a phone that's completely dead with no charging response at all. This is micro-soldering territory — exactly the work our bench is set up for, and diagnosis is free on most repairs.
