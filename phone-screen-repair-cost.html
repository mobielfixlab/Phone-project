The four things that change the price

1. Your phone model

Newer and bigger costs more. A 3-year-old iPhone's display is plentiful and cheap; this year's Pro Max panel isn't. That's most of the spread between $49 and $149+.

2. LCD vs. OLED

Budget phones use LCD panels, flagships use OLED. OLED replacements cost more as parts — same labor, pricier glass. Some models offer a choice: a quality aftermarket LCD (cheaper) or an OLED matching the original (pricier but identical to factory look).

3. Part grade

This is the one shops don't always explain, and it's where "too good to be true" quotes come from. Screens come in grades — economy aftermarket, premium aftermarket, and genuine service-pack panels. A $30 difference in quote is usually a whole grade of difference in part. Always ask which grade you're getting. We tell you up front and price each option.

4. Hidden damage

Occasionally a "cracked screen" is also a bent frame or a damaged connector underneath. A proper diagnosis catches this before you commit — free on most repairs at our lab.

How to spot a fair quote (anywhere, not just here)

  • Firm price before work starts — never "we'll see once it's open."
  • Part grade stated plainly — genuine, premium aftermarket, or economy.
  • Warranty included — 90 days on parts and workmanship is a healthy standard; ours covers most repairs.
  • Diagnosis included — free on most repairs; you shouldn't pay just to learn the price.
💡

Repair vs. new phone: if your phone is under 4–5 years old, repair wins on math almost every time — $49–$149 versus several hundred dollars, and you keep your data, apps and settings untouched. See what repairs look like on our before & after page.

What about insurance or AppleCare?

If you have AppleCare+ or carrier insurance with a low screen-repair deductible, use it — that's what you paid for. Where independent shops like ours win is no deductible, no claim, no shipping your phone away: walk in, wait 45 minutes, walk out. For out-of-warranty devices, an independent repair is nearly always the cheaper path.
