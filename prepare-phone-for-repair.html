Repair tips · Preparation

What should I do before
taking my phone in?

Five minutes of prep protects your data and gets you a faster, more accurate repair. Here's the checklist we'd give our own family.

By the Mobile Fix Lab technicians Updated July 2026 4 min read

1. Back up your data — even though repairs don't touch it

Here's the honest truth: a normal hardware repair (screen, battery, charging port) never touches your photos, messages or apps. But a phone that's already damaged is a phone that can fail without warning — a cracked screen can stop displaying entirely, a failing battery can refuse to power on. Back up before that happens:

  • iPhone: Settings → your name → iCloud → iCloud Backup → Back Up Now (on Wi-Fi).
  • Android: Settings → Google → Backup → Back up now.
  • If the screen is too broken to use, don't worry — tell the shop. We can often complete a repair without touching your data at all, and screens can usually be replaced with data fully intact.

2. Know your passcode situation

You don't have to share your passcode for most hardware repairs. A good shop will only ask to use it briefly — with your permission — to test the parts they replaced (touch response, cameras, speakers). If you'd rather not share it:

  • Ask for the function test to be done together with you at pickup, or
  • Temporarily set a simple passcode (like 0000) for the repair and change it back after.

Any shop that demands your passcode for a basic screen or battery job without explaining why is a red flag.

3. Write down the symptoms

The more specific you can be, the faster the diagnosis — and diagnosis time is what you often pay for. Before you come in, note:

  • What happened: dropped, sat on, got wet, just stopped working?
  • When it started and whether it's constant or intermittent.
  • Anything you already tried: restarts, different chargers, a DIY fix.
💧

Water damage is the exception to everything: don't charge it, don't keep testing it, skip the rice myth — just power it off and bring it in as soon as possible. Corrosion spreads by the hour, and early ultrasonic cleaning is what saves water-damaged phones.

4. Remove your case and screen protector

Take off the case and any screen protector before you arrive (a shattered protector can hide the real screen condition). Your SIM card can stay in the phone. If the repair is for a charging problem, bring your cable and charger too — sometimes they're the actual fault, and that's a much cheaper fix.

5. Know what a fair shop should offer you

Walking in prepared also means knowing what to expect. At a trustworthy repair shop you should get:

  • A free or low-cost diagnosis with a firm quote before any work starts. (Ours is free on most repairs.)
  • A clear time estimate — most screens and batteries are 30–60 minute jobs, so ask if you can wait for it.
  • A written warranty on parts and workmanship. We give 90 days on most repairs.
  • The choice between part grades (genuine vs. premium aftermarket) where both exist, with honest pricing for each.

The quick version

  1. Back up (iCloud / Google).
  2. Decide how you want to handle your passcode.
  3. Note what happened and what you've tried.
  4. Remove case + screen protector; bring your charger if charging is the issue.
  5. Expect a fair diagnosis (free on most repairs), a firm quote, time estimate and warranty.
