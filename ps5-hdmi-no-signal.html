PS5 on, TV says
"no signal"?

Nine times out of ten that's a damaged HDMI port — and it's very fixable. Here's how to tell, what not to try, and what the repair costs.

By the Mobile Fix Lab technicians Updated July 2026 4 min read

First: rule out the cheap stuff (2 minutes)

  1. Try a different HDMI cable. Cables fail more often than ports. Borrow one you know works.
  2. Try another HDMI input on your TV, and another TV if you can.
  3. Boot the PS5 in Safe Mode (hold the power button until the second beep) and pick "Change Video Output" — this fixes resolution-mismatch black screens.

If a good cable on a good TV still gets nothing, keep reading.

How to tell the port itself is damaged

Grab a flashlight and look straight into the console's HDMI port. Healthy ports have a neat row of identical gold pins. Signs of damage:

  • Bent, flattened or missing pins
  • The center tongue is cracked or sitting crooked
  • The cable feels loose, or only works if you hold it at an angle
  • Bits of plastic or metal debris inside the port

This damage usually comes from the console being pulled by the cable, tipping over while plugged in, or someone forcing a cable in upside down. It's the single most common console repair on our bench.

⚠️

Don't try to bend pins back with tweezers. The pins connect to solder joints on the motherboard — levering them can rip the pads off the board and turn a routine repair into a dead console. And a damaged port can also stress the IC chip behind it the longer you keep re-plugging.

What the repair involves (and costs)

An HDMI port isn't a plug-in part — it's micro-soldered to the motherboard. A proper repair means opening the console, removing the board, desoldering the damaged port with hot air, cleaning the pads under a microscope, and soldering on a new port.

At our Mint Hill lab, console HDMI port repair starts at $119 for PS5, PS4 and Xbox Series consoles and typically takes 1–2 days. Compare that with the cost of a new console — and remember your saves, accounts and storage all stay exactly as they were.

While it's open, we check the rest

Consoles that come in for HDMI work are usually a few years old and full of dust. We inspect the fan and thermal paste while we're in there — if your console was also getting loud or hot, mention it and we'll quote a cleaning at the same time. (Running hot? See our console shutdown guide.)
