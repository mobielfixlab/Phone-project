PS5 & Xbox HDMI port repair
in Mint Hill, NC.

Console turns on but the TV says "no signal"? We replace damaged HDMI ports with proper board-level micro-soldering — from $119, done in 1–2 days, saves and accounts untouched. Get a free quote.

The classic symptoms

  • Console powers on (lights, fan) but the TV reports no signal
  • Picture only works when the cable is held at an angle
  • The HDMI cable feels loose or wobbly in the console
  • You can see bent or broken pins inside the port with a flashlight

Before you bring it in, rule out the cheap stuff: try a known-good cable and a different TV input. Still nothing? It's almost certainly the port — the single most common console repair on our bench. Our PS5 "no signal" guide walks through the full diagnosis.

A real repair, not a workaround

The HDMI port isn't a plug-in part — it's micro-soldered to the motherboard with dozens of tiny connections. Our repair means opening the console, removing the board, desoldering the damaged port with hot air, cleaning the pads under a microscope, fitting a new port, and testing the console end-to-end before it goes back together.

⚠️

Don't bend the pins back yourself. Levering inside the port can rip the solder pads off the motherboard — that turns a routine $119 repair into a console that can't be saved.

Repair beats replacing — by a lot

HDMI port repair starts at $119 and takes 1–2 days. Compare that with the cost of a new console — and your saves, accounts, controllers and installed games all stay exactly as they were. While the console is open we also check the fan and thermal paste; if it's been running loud or hot, mention it and we'll quote a cleaning at the same time.

Will my saves and account be safe?

Yes. We're replacing a physical port on the motherboard — your storage isn't wiped or touched, so saves, accounts and installed games stay exactly as they were.

How do I know it's the port and not the cable or TV?

Try a known-good cable and a different TV input first. If the console powers on but still shows no signal — or the cable feels loose — the port is the likely culprit. We confirm it with a free diagnosis on most repairs.
