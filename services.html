FAQ

Good questions.

How long does a typical repair take?

Screens and batteries are usually done in 30–60 minutes. Water damage and more complex repairs take 1–3 days depending on severity. We'll always give you a time estimate up front.

Do you use original parts?

We stock genuine and premium-grade parts and will always tell you exactly which is being fitted, with pricing for both where available.

Is my data safe during repair?

Yes. We never need your passcode for hardware repairs unless testing requires it (we'll ask first). Your data never leaves your device, and we're happy to repair with data privacy mode on request.

What does the warranty cover?

Most repairs include a 90-day warranty covering the part we installed and the work we performed. Accidental damage after the repair isn't covered — but we'll always look after you on price.

What if you can't fix it?

Diagnostics are free on most repairs — if we can't fix it, you won't pay for the attempt.

How much does phone screen repair cost?

Most phone screens run $49–$149 depending on the model, LCD vs OLED, and the part grade you choose. iPhone screens start at $49, quoted firmly before any work starts. Full pricing guide →

What should I do before bringing my phone in?

Back up your data, remove your case and screen protector, and note what happened. You don't need to share your passcode for most hardware repairs. Full preparation checklist →

Should I try a DIY repair kit first?

For an old spare phone and a simple job, honestly — maybe! For a daily phone, glued batteries, water damage or board faults, a shop is safer and often cheaper. Our honest DIY vs. shop guide →
