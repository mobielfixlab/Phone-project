Phone took a swim?
The clock is running.

What you do in the first 10 minutes decides whether a wet phone lives. Here's the emergency checklist — and why the rice trick quietly kills phones.

By the Mobile Fix Lab technicians Updated July 2026 4 min read

Do this right now

  1. Get it out and power it off. Hold the power button and shut it down completely. Electricity flowing through wet circuits is what causes shorts — a powered-off wet phone is in far less danger.
  2. Do NOT plug it in. Charging a wet phone is the fastest way to fry the charging circuit. Ignore the urge to "check if it still works."
  3. Strip it. Case off, SIM tray out, screen protector off if water got under it.
  4. Dab, don't shake. Dry the outside with a cloth. Hold the ports facing down and gently tap the water out. Don't blow into it — that pushes water deeper.
  5. No heat. No hair dryers, radiators or ovens. Heat drives moisture deeper and warps adhesives and the battery.
  6. Get it to a shop — fast. This is the step that actually saves phones. Corrosion starts within hours.
🍚

The rice myth, retired: rice barely pulls moisture from inside a sealed phone. What actually happens during those three days in the rice bag is corrosion — minerals left by the water eat away at the board while you wait. Even Apple has told people not to do it. Rice costs you the exact hours that decide whether the phone survives.

Why phones die days later

Here's what the "it still works!" crowd misses: water doesn't usually kill a phone instantly. The minerals it leaves behind corrode the board gradually — the phone works fine for days or weeks, then the screen flickers, the battery drains weirdly, and one morning it doesn't turn on. By then the corrosion has spread. A phone that got properly wet deserves an inspection even if it seems fine.

What we actually do about it

Professional water damage treatment isn't drying — it's cleaning. We open the device, disconnect the battery, and run the board through an ultrasonic cleaner that strips corrosion and mineral deposits out of places no cloth can reach, then inspect under the microscope and repair any damaged components. At our Mint Hill lab it starts at $79 with results in 24–48 hours — and the earlier the phone arrives, the better the save rate.

"But my phone is water resistant"

IP ratings are tested with fresh water, on new phones, in lab conditions. Age, drops and worn seals lower the real-world protection — and pool, ocean and toilet water are far harsher than the test tank. Water resistance buys you time to follow the steps above; it isn't immunity.
